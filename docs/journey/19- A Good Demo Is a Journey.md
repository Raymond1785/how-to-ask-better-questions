## 19. A Good Demo Is a Journey

When I first started building my side project, I thought a good demo meant showing everything I had built.

More functions.

More proof that the system could do a lot.

But most product demos feel like a tour of the system.

Click here.

Open this page.

Show another function.

That may explain how the product works.

But it does not explain why people should care.

What would make people pay attention?

What would make them want to see the next step?

It is like starting a conversation with someone you have never met.

You do not begin by telling them everything about yourself.

You first give them a reason to keep listening.

Then I realized:

**A good demo is not a tour of the product.**

**It is a journey through the audience’s attention.**

**A good demo should begin with the audience, not the product.**
