## 12. Build for Wow, Not Just Work

When building something, the first goal is usually simple:

**Make it work.**

The data loads.

The calculation is correct.

The button does what it should.

Technically, the job is done.

But then someone sees it and says:

**“Okay.”**

That was when I realized something.

People rarely remember how many functions you built.

They remember the moment when they suddenly think:

**“Wow. I didn't know it could do that.”**

That does not always require more features.

Sometimes, it is simply about showing the right thing at the right moment.

A good product should work.

But if you want people to remember it:

**Build for the moment when “It works” becomes “Wow.”**
