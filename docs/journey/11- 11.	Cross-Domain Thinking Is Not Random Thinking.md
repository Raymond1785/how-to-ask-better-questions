## 11. Cross-Domain Thinking Is Not Random Thinking

When we face a problem, we usually look for answers in the same field.

That makes sense.

But it also means we often find the same answers everyone else has already found.

Sometimes, the better solution comes from somewhere completely different.

A problem in software may have already been solved in manufacturing.

A problem in data may have already been solved in healthcare.

A problem in decision-making may have already been solved in another industry.

There is a line from *Now You See Me* that captures this perfectly:

**“The closer you look, the less you see.”**

Sometimes, we are simply too close to our own field to see the problem clearly.

**The goal is not to randomly collect ideas from different fields.**

The goal is to ask:

**Where else does the same kind of problem exist?**

Then bring the useful part back.

That is why cross-domain thinking matters.

**Sometimes you need to leave your field to see your own problem differently.**
