## 10. Real Users Are the Best Designers

We designed my side project based on what we thought people would need.

Then we started using it.

That changed everything.

Things that looked clear while building the system became confusing in actual use.

Features we thought were important were sometimes ignored.

And small problems we had never considered suddenly mattered.

Real use kept showing us what our design had missed.

Not because users knew how to design the system better.

But because they knew where the system failed to help them.

Every question, hesitation, and workaround became a design signal.

We learned that the best design ideas do not always come from sitting down and trying to design better.

**Sometimes, they come from watching where real users struggle.**

**Real users may not design the product for you.  
But they will tell you what is inconvenient for them.**
