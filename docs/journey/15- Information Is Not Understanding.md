## 15. Information Is Not Understanding

At one point, I thought that if a system showed enough information, people would understand what was happening.

More charts.  
More indicators.

But information is not the same as understanding.

A screen can contain everything and still leave one question unanswered:

**What is my next step?**

That changed how I started thinking about information systems.

The real challenge was not how much information we could collect or display.

It was whether the system could help people see:

- Why does it matter?
- What should I look at next?

The more data we added, the more important this became.

Because when everything is visible, nothing is necessarily clear.

A useful system should not just give people more information.

**It should reduce the distance between seeing the data and understanding what it means.**
